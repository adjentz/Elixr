Actions

Any physical activity a creature wants to perform is referred to as an action. Typical actions include: Attacking, Travelling, Skill Checks, and Casting Spells. There are also Special Actions referred to as Maneuvers that have special rules and are described below.

Talking during combat has no Speed Cost, but whatever is said must be able to be stated within 6-seconds.

Attacking

Making an Attack always has a Speed Cost of 30ft.

To make an Attack, you roll an Attack Roll.

An Attack Roll is 1d20 + The Modifier of the Weapon's Attack Ability + Any Misc. Bonuses.

If the result of an Attack roll is greater than the target's Defense, the attack succeeds and a Damage Roll is made.

A Damage Roll is the damage of the weapon being wielded + the Modifier of the weapon's Damage Ability.

On a successful attack, the target of the attack reduces their Energy by the result of the Damage Roll.

If the target had any Channeled Energy, their Energy is further reduced by the amount of Channeled Energy they have, if any, and then the Channeled Energy is reset to 0.

See the next section for what happens when Energy is reduced to 0 or below, as well as how to recover Energy.

Travelling

The simplest way to use Movement Speed is to walk or run.

The Speed Cost of travelling on the ground in any direction is equal to the distance travelled.

Therefore, moving 5ft consumes 5ft of Movement Speed. Moving 10ft consumes 10ft of Movement speed, and so on.

The Grid

It is often useful to imagine combat occurring on a grid consisting of 5'x5' squares.

A Medium-sized humanoid creature occupies a single square on the grid, and advancing forward one square in any direction, including diagonally, has a Speed Cost of 5ft.

Some larger creatures take up more space on the grid than one square. How much space is determined by their size Feature.

Other creatures are so small that more than one of that type of creature can occupy the same square. How many can occupy the same square is determined by their size Feature.

Skill Checks

The description of a skill check indicates the Speed Cost of the skill.

Using a Skill in combat is typically no different than using a skill outside of combat.

Casting a Spell

The description of a spell indicates the Speed Cost required in order to cast it.

Once the Speed Cost has been applied to your character's Movement Speed, the spell effect is manifested.

Casting a spell in combat is typically no different than using a spell outside of combat, other than a character should be wary that being hit while they have Channeled Energy will have repercussions detailed on the Magic page.

Some spells call for a Spell Attack roll. This is 1d20 + your Focus Bonus + Any other bonuses from Features or enchanted items.

If the Spell Attack roll result is greater than the targets Defense, you can roll damage as indicated by the spell description.

Other Actions

For any other actions a player wants to attempt, a Speed Cost is assigned by the GM. Here are some common examples and what their Speed Cost might be:
Action Speed Cost
Drop Weapon 0ft
Sheath/Unsheath Weapon 5ft
Drink a Potion 15ft
Stand up from prone. 20ft

Delay Action

Sometimes you want to perform your actions after another event has occurred.

You can forgo your turn on your Initiative, and instead take your turn at the end of someone else's Initiative.

This is called Delaying Action. On the next round, your turn occurs at your original Initiative.

Readying an Action

Sometimes you know something is going to occur, but can't react to it yet because it hasn't happened.

In these cases you may Ready an Action by stating what action you will perform on what trigger.

For example, you may say "I wait next to this wall, and when he comes around the corner, I attack him." This has a trigger: A target comes around a corner. It also has an Action: Attack the target.

When stating your trigger, it should be specific. If you state you attack anyone the comes around the corner, you may end up hitting an ally unless you state that you'll check that the target is an enemy.

When the trigger occurs, the Readied Action is resolved immediately, even if it is not your turn.

Maneuvers

Maneuvers are special actions that can be performed in combat. The maneuvers are: Shove, Trip, Grab, and Disarm.

To perform a Maneuver against a creature, an Athletics check is rolled. If the result exceeds the target's Athletics Defense, or the target's Acrobatics Defense, which ever is higher, the Maneuver succeeds.

All Maneuvers have a Speed Cost of 30ft, which is applied whether or not the Maneuever is successful.

What happens when a Maneuver succeeds depends on the Maneuever.

Shove

On a successful Shove Maneuver, the target is pushed back 5ft, or 1 square.

Trip

On a successful Trip Maneuver, the target falls to the ground, prone. The following conditions apply to a prone creature:

  • Melee attacks made against the creature have Advantage.
  • Ranged attacks made against the creature have Disadvantage.
  • The creature may attack from the ground, but with Disadvantage.
  • The creature can crawl or stand up. Crawling has a Speed Cost of 10ft for every 5ft travelled.

Grab

On a successful Grab Maneuver, the creature performing the Maneuver holds the target in place.

Both the Grabber and Grabbed are considered Grappled. The following conditions apply to both Grappled creatures:

  • When an attack is made against a creature that is Grappled, there is a 50% chance of hitting the other creature.
  • Both creatures share the same square as the creature that performed the Grab Maneuver.
  • A Grappled creature cannot walk or run.
  • A Grappled creature cannot cast a spell, unless they are allowed to cast the spell without the need to move their hands.
  • A Grappled creature can make an attack against the creature they are engaged in a Grapple with.
  • A Grappled creature can attempt a Maneuever against the creature they are engaged in. If they succeed in the Maneuever, the Grab ends, and the Maneuever result is applied.

Disarm

On a successful Grab Maneuver, the target of the Maneuver drops their weapon.

If the creature performing the Maneuver had no weapon in their hands when attempting the maneuever, they may choose to have the weapon in their hands instead of it falling to the floor.
