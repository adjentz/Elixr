Skills

Skills indicate how adept or well trained your character is at performing a certain action. Each Skill is detailed below.

Skills have four parts: Ranks, Modifier, Misc., and Bonus.

Ranks

You determine how versatile you want your character to be at a Skill by placing points in the ranks column.

When you first create your character, each rank starts at 0. You then have a pool of 8 Points to distribute between all of the Skills. To increase a rank by one, you reduce the pool by one, until the remaining Skill points is zero.

Ranks for a Skill can go no higher than your character's level + 3

Modifier

The Skill modifier is carried down from the relevant ability modifier. If your Strength Modifier is 3, for example, all of the Skills in the Strength section have a modifier of 3. The same follows for the other abilities.

Misc.

Various Features and magical items can give a Misc. bonus to specific Skills. This is added to the Misc. bonus of the relevent Ability.

Bonus

The sum of Ranks, Modifier, and Misc..

Making a Skill check

Your GM may call for a Skill check to be rolled when you attempt an action related to that Skill.

To roll a Skill check, take the result of 1d20 + Skill Bonus. Your GM will tell you if the result is high enough to perform the action you were attempting.

Regardless of if the check succeeds or not, the Speed cost, if any, is applied fully.

Criticaling

When rolling 1d20, if the result of the roll is 20 before any other modifiers are applied, this is referred to as a critical.

A critical indicates an automatic success, as long as the GM rules that the action isn't physically impossible, and that you have the necessary equipment, if any.

A critical also means that you performed the action so effectively, that you can add a flourish to the action for flavor, if desired.

Fumbling

When rolling 1d20, if the result of the roll is 1 before any other modifiers are applied, this is referred to as a fumble.

A fumble indicates an automatic failure, but only if a creature is under pressure, such as a combat situation. Otherwise, the roll is added normally.

On a fumble, you can still continue your turn, even to retry the check, as long as you have enough Speed remaining to do so.

Opposed Skill checks

When certain Skills are attempted against a creature, that creature has the opportunity to roll a corresponding opposing Skill check, instead of a Difficulty assigned by the GM. These are specified in the Skill description.

Strength Skills

Skills that fall under the Strength ability.

Athletics

Athletics is the Skill to perform general feats of athleticism, such as jumping over a chasm or throwing a grappling hook.

  • Speed Cost: Varies
  • On failure: Varies

Climb

Climb is the Skill to scale difficult surfaces.

  • Speed Cost: 10ft for every 5ft climbed.
  • On failure: The creature remains in the same spot.

Swim

Swim is the Skill to move through water quickly and effecively. Calm water does not require a check.

  • Speed Cost: 10ft for every 5ft swam.
  • On failure: The creature begins to sink.

Agility Skills

Skills that fall under the Agility ability.

Acrobatics

Acrobatics is the Skill to balance on a ledge, tumble between a giant's legs, dodge out of harm's way, and recuce fall damage.

  • Speed Cost: Varies
  • On failure: Varies

Escape Artist

Escape Artist is the Skill to escape restraints such as handcuffs and knots.

  • Speed Cost: 30ft
  • On failure: The creature remains restrained.

Sleight of Hand

Sleight of Hand is the Skill to cleverly move one's hands so as to conceal their actions, such as hiding a dagger in their boot, or slipping poison into the Elf's drink.

  • Speed Cost: 5ft
  • Oppossed by: Perception check
  • On failure: If the Sleight of Hand check does not exceed the target's Perception check, the target notices the action the creature is attempting, and can react to it.

Stealth

Stealth is this Skill to move and hide without being noticed.

  • Speed Cost: 10ft for every 5ft travelled.
  • Opposed by: Perception check
  • On failure: If the Stealth check does not exceed the target's perception check, the target becomes aware of the creature's presence.

Focus Skills

Skills that fall under the Focus ability.

Concentration

Concentration is the Skill to not be distracted, as well as protect your mind from certain spells.

  • Speed Cost: None
  • On failure: Varies

Engineer

Engineer is the Skill to create items and discern structural details.

  • Speed Cost: Varies
  • On failure: The result is ineffective.

Insight

Insight is the Skill to discern information from subtle details.

  • Speed Cost: None
  • On failure: No extra information is discovered.

Medicine

Medicine is the Skill to determine a creatures ailments and apply cures to some wounds.

  • Speed Cost: Varies, at least 60ft.
  • On failure: The treatment is ineffective.

Perception

Perception is the Skill to notice the presence or absence of other entites through sound, sight, smell, or other sense.

  • Speed Cost: None.
  • On failure: Something goes unnoticed.

Recall

Recall is the Skill to remember information that a creature would have collected in the past. The information must be something a creature would already know. The GM can challenge if a creature would actually have the information depending on their background.

  • Speed Cost: None
  • On failure: The information isn't available to the creature. A retry can only be attempted a day later.

Survival

Survival is the Skill to survive in the wild. Can include things such as tying knots in a rope, lighting a fire, or discerning what direction is North.

  • Speed Cost: Varies
  • On failure: The action is unsuccessful.

Charm Skills

Skills that fall under the Charm ability.

Animal Handling

Animal Handling is the Skill to effectively interact with animals. A successful check will shift an animals attitude by one step in a positive direction.

  • Speed Cost: Varies, at least 60ft.
  • On failure: If the check fails by a difference of 5 or more, the animal's attitude shifts one step in a negative direction. Otherwise the animal remains at it's previous stance towards the creature.

Deception

Deception is the Skill to bluff and misdirect others. If a lie is too unbelievable, a GM may allow the target to roll with Advantage, or increase the Difficulty of performing the Deception.

  • Speed Cost: None
  • Opposed by: Insight check.
  • On failure: If the Deception check does not exceed the target's Insight check, the target begins to doubt the creature.

Diplomacy

Diplomacy is the Skill to cause other to see your point of view. A successful check will shift another's attitude towards the creature by one step in a postive direction.

  • Speed Cost: None
  • Opposed by:Diplomacy check
  • On failure: If the check fails by a difference of 5 or more, the target's attitude shifts one step in a negative direction. Otherwise the target remains at it's previous stance towards the creature.

Diplomacy cannot be used to coerce a creature into taking an action that would be against their character. Player characters cannot be influenced with Diplomacy checks.

Perform

Perform is the Skill to effectively capture the attention of others through song, stories, dance, or playing an instrument. On exceptional checks, the audience may even tip the performer.

  • Speed Cost: Varies, at least 60ft
  • On failure: The audience doesn't pay much attention to the performer.
